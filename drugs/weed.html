how to grow weed

outdoors

the plants do need a minimum of eight hours of sunlight per day and should be planted in late april. of course, an outdoors setting has difficulties

not encountered with an indoors crop. you must be able to avoid detection both from law enforcement and people, both of

whom will take your weed and probably use it. also, one will also arrest you. you must also have access to the area to prepare the soil

and harvest the crop. there are two ways of planting the seeds. first one is - you should plant seeds in a box or something for about ten days

and then transplant. the other one is - you should just plant them in the correct location. (this one is worse cuz the plants might die)

the soil should be prepared for weed by turning it over a couple of times and adding about one cup of hydrated lime per square yard of soil

and a little bit of water soluble nitrogen fertilizer. the soil should now be watered several times and left to sit about one week.

the plants should be planted at least three feet apart, stacking them too close will result in crippled plants.

the plants like some water during their growing season, but not too much. especially around the roots, too much water will rot the root system.

grass grows well in corn or hops, these plants will help provide some camouflage.

it is probably a good idea to plant in many small, broken patches, as people tend to notice patterns.

indoors

indoor growing has many advantages, besides the apparent fact that it is much harder to have your weed found, you can control the ambient

conditions just exactly as you want them and get a guaranteed good plant. plants grown indoors will not the same as outdoor plants.

they will be skinnier appearing with weak stems and may even require you to tie them to a growing post to remain upright, BUT

THEY WILL HAVE AS MUCH OR MORE RESIN!!!11! if growing in a room, you should put tar paper on the floors and then buy sterilized bags of soil

from a nursery or something. you will need about one cubic foot of soil for each plant. the plants will need about 150 ml of water per plant per week.

they will also need fresh air, so the room must be ventilated. at least eight hours of light a day must be provided.

as you increase the light, the plants grow faster and show more females and less males. sixteen hours of light per day seems to be the best,

anything more makes no difference in the plant quality. another idea is to interrupt the night cycle with about one hour of light. this gives you more females.

the walls of your growing room should be painted white or covered with aluminum foil to reflect the light.

the lights can be either bulbs of fluorescent. figure about 75 watts per plant or one plant per two feet of fluorescent tube.

the fluorescents are the best, but do not use the "weird cool white" types. the light sources should be an average of twenty inches from the plant

BUT NEVER closer than 14 inches.

both the male and he female plants produce thc resin (male be shitty).
